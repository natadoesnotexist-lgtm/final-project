🏰 Who were the Vikings?

The Vikings were seafaring people from Scandinavia (present-day Denmark, Norway, and Sweden) who from the late 8th to late 11th centuries raided, traded, and settled across Europe. Their legacy shaped the British Isles, France, Russia, and even reached North America — 500 years before Columbus.

⚓ Expert Shipbuilders & Navigators

Their iconic longships were fast, shallow‑draft vessels that could navigate open seas and shallow rivers. This allowed them to strike deep inland, from Paris to Constantinople. The Vikings established trade routes stretching from Greenland to the Caspian Sea.

🗡️ Not Just Raiders – Farmers, Traders & Lawmakers

Contrary to myth, most Vikings were peaceful farmers, craftsmen, and merchants. They created the Thing – one of the earliest democratic assemblies. They also brought ironworking, advanced textiles, and the runic alphabet (Elder Futhark).

🛡️ Religion & Mythology

Before Christianity, Vikings worshipped gods like Odin, Thor, Freyja, and Loki. Their myths predicted Ragnarök – the end of the world. Many Viking burial sites contain weapons, ships, and treasures meant for the afterlife.

🏆 Viking Age Legacy

The Viking Age ended around 1066 AD with the Norman conquest of England – ironically led by descendants of Vikings (Normans). Yet their influence remains in language (Thursday = Thor’s day), place names, and the enduring fascination with Norse culture.
